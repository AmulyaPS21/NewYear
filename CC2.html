Fun Facts about New Year

Last updated - 12/23/2021
1.The Babylonians celebrated the start of the new year on the first new moon following the vernal equinox, the day in late march with an equal amount of daylight and darkness.

2.The ancient Romans celebrated the new year in March when planting crops began.

3.Julius Caesar, after whom the Julian calendar is named, marked the New Year as January 1st in 46 B.C.

4.Until 755, the French began the year on December 25, then moved it to March first, and then in the Twelfth Century the year began at Easter. King Charles IX declared January first as the start of the new year in 1564.

5.March 25th, the Day of the Annunciation was celebrated as New Year by the English until 1752 when they decided to make January 1st the first day of the New Year.

6.In Alaska, Eskimos celebrate the New Year’s Day with the arrival of the first snow.

7.In Israel, a European-style New Year’s Day is celebrated on January 1st, and they have another celebration in September.
